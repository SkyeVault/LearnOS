# LearnOS teaching workflow screencast

This is the current recording script for the local Teacher Desk walkthrough. Record at 1440×900 or larger and use a fresh local profile so the empty states are visible.

## 0:00 — Local-first teaching home

Open LearnOS at `http://localhost:1420`. Sign in as an administrator and briefly show the People & access workspace. Explain that accounts, teaching records, classes, modules, and resource uploads remain on the local device.

## 0:20 — People, access, and learning setup

Open a learner record. Show the editable core record, permanent family links, assigned classes, Learning modules, and Learning spaces & evidence. Toggle an installed module and a learning-space checkbox, then save. Point out that an administrator can create learners and staff, edit names, stages, roles, sign-in credentials, and build a class with an enrolled roster from a teacher’s record.

## 0:55 — Teacher Desk and class setup

Sign in as a teacher. From Classroom home, create or review a class and its learner roster. Explain that the class is the shared anchor for lesson plans, the calendar, evidence, messages, and resource organization.

## 1:15 — Real weekly calendar

At the top of Classroom home, show the MUI weekly scheduler. Create a dated plan from Plan & outlines, return to Classroom home, and show it on the calendar. Drag or edit an event to demonstrate that the schedule is a working weekly view rather than a static progress list.

## 1:50 — Living Library as the starting point

Show the Living Library card on the Teacher Desk and open it. Explain the teaching loop: discover a source, adapt it into a lesson outline or material, then document learning evidence.

## 2:10 — Teaching Reference and the private resource shelf

Open Teaching library. Start with Teaching Reference, then scroll directly below it to **My local resource shelf**. Create a folder such as “Seed unit,” choose a class, upload a small PDF, image, or document, and show it listed with its folder, class, size, Open, and Remove controls. Explain that the shelf is controlled by the signed-in teacher and is stored locally; it accepts files up to 4 MB.

## 2:45 — Teaching record, mastery, and communication

Open Teaching record. Show the class-scoped daily log, skill/mastery evidence, and the workflow for sending an assignment or message to one learner or an entire class. Mention that spreadsheet imports use a safe browser-side reader for supported CSV/XLSX material and remain local.

## 3:15 — Close

Return to the Teacher Desk calendar and resource shelf. Close with: “LearnOS keeps the gradebook, schedule, lesson source, classroom journal, materials, and family communication close to the teacher—so the teacher can stay with the class.”

## Recording notes

- Do not show real student names, messages, passwords, or uploaded records.
- Use a small sample file for the upload segment so the save is immediate.
- If the scheduler was just installed or updated, restart the LearnOS server on port 1420 before recording.
